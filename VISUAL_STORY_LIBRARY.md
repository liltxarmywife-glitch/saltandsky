# Salt & Sky — Visual Story Library

## The Shift
We are not building a website. We are building a visual story.

Every image answers one question:
> "What kind of memories are waiting for me here?"

Images are organized by **emotion and story** — not by page.
When building or updating any page, choose images that best tell that page's emotional story.

---

## The Library (Target: 200–250 images)

### 1. People & Relationships (60–80 images)
The heart of Salt & Sky. Nobody looking at the camera.

**Families**
- Grandparent + grandchild discovering the beach together
- Parents watching children run into waves (backs to camera)
- Multigenerational family around a long outdoor dinner table
- Grandfather showing grandson whales from a ship deck
- Kids building sandcastles while grandparent watches from nearby

**Couples**
- Walking barefoot at sunrise, backs to camera
- Coffee on a balcony overlooking water, talking to each other
- Dancing on the beach at sunset, backs to camera
- Sitting quietly watching the horizon together
- Couple in their 50s laughing at something we can't hear

**Friends**
- Toasting around a fire pit, nobody looking at camera
- Women walking down a resort path together, backs to camera, laughing
- Long dinner table outdoors, mid-conversation, candles, string lights
- Sitting on a dock with feet over the water

**The Salt & Sky Team**
- Mel and Kelsey walking together, coffee in hand, laughing
- Looking at something in the distance, not at camera
- Planning at a table, heads down, animated conversation
- NOT headshots — candid, unmistakably them

---

### 2. Destinations & Landscapes (30–40 images)
Each destination needs TWO images: the place AND the memory inside it.

**Caribbean**
- Turquoise bay, sailboat, palm trees, golden hour
- Grandparents watching grandchildren discover the beach

**Bahamas**
- Shallow crystal water over white sand
- Couple walking barefoot at sunrise

**Mexico**
- Luxury infinity pool overlooking the coast
- Family at the pool, kids playing, parents watching

**Costa Rica**
- Lush rainforest waterfall or volcano with mist
- Couple on suspension bridge or ziplining through canopy

**Alaska**
- Glacier or fjord from a ship deck, dramatic and moody
- Grandfather showing grandson whales from the deck

---

### 3. Quiet Moments (20–30 images)
These slow the page down. Used as section dividers and breathing room.

**Morning**
- Coffee cup on a railing, ocean in background
- Empty breakfast table on villa balcony, golden light
- Sunrise over water, no people

**Reflection**
- Person reading in a hammock, face away
- Walking alone on the beach at dawn
- Journal open on a table with ocean view
- Watching waves from a chair

**Details**
- Sandals left on the beach
- A child's hand in an adult's hand
- Two glasses of wine, nobody holding them, sunset behind
- Ship porthole with ocean view

---

### 4. Celebrations (30–40 images)
The reason people book these trips.

- Milestone birthday dinner outdoors, mid-laugh, candles
- Anniversary couple receiving a surprise, pure joy
- Graduation on the beach (cap and gown, family surrounding)
- Family reunion spread across a beach lawn, candid clusters
- Proposal moment (the reaction, not the setup)
- Retirement sendoff, group of colleagues laughing together
- Bachelorette — women walking together, not holding drinks to camera
- Church group gathered outdoors, heads bowed or in conversation

---

### 5. Luxury Details (20–30 images)
Aspirational. No people required.

- Ship deck at sunset, empty chairs facing the water
- White linens, open balcony door, ocean beyond
- Champagne glasses on a railing, water in background
- Elegant place setting at an outdoor dinner
- Room service tray with coffee and pastries, balcony view
- Pool with infinity edge, no one in it, golden hour

---

### 6. Adventures (20–30 images)
Action and wonder — but still candid, not posed.

- Ziplining through rainforest canopy
- Snorkeling — feet at the surface, coral below
- Kayaking in a turquoise bay, backs to camera
- Whale tail above water, ship railing in foreground
- Hiking a coastal trail, figures small in the landscape
- Child seeing the ocean for the first time

---

### 7. Seasonal & Holiday Travel (20–30 images)
People travel for the calendar too.

- Christmas cruise — family gathering on a ship deck, twinkling lights
- New Year's at sea — fireworks reflected in water
- Thanksgiving table outdoors in a tropical setting
- Summer family reunion, golden afternoon light
- Spring break — young families at the beach, natural and candid

---

## Recurring Visual Themes
These emotions appear across EVERY page:

| Theme | Visual |
|---|---|
| Morning | Coffee, balcony, sunrise, quiet |
| Connection | Hands, laughter, conversation, children |
| Celebration | Dinner tables, candles, cheers (not to camera) |
| Wonder | Ocean, mountains, sunsets, whales, palms |
| Reflection | Walking, reading, watching waves |

---

## The Rule (Always)
> If the image could be mistaken for a stock travel advertisement, we don't use it.
> Every image should make someone think: **"I wish that were my memory."**
